Pork shoulder in a hoisin-honey char siu glaze, roasted Sunday, and rice cooked Sunday specifically so it can dry out — because fried rice made from fresh rice is porridge, and fried rice made from three-day-old rice is the reason wok burners exist. This dinner is meal prep arguing from strength: both components are actively better for having waited.

## Ingredients

- 2 lb | pork shoulder | meat | a chunk off the Costco two-pack, cut into two long strips
- 160 g | hoisin sauce | other-store | about 0.5 cup; Lee Kum Kee if choosing
- 555 g | jasmine rice | pantry | measured dry, about 3 cups; cooked Sunday, deliberately staled in the fridge
- 4 | large eggs | dairy-eggs
- 200 g | frozen peas | frozen | about 1.5 cups
- 2 | carrots | produce
- 1 bunch | green onions | produce
- 4 cloves | garlic | produce
- 1 oz | fresh ginger | produce

### Pantry staples

- soy sauce
- honey
- toasted sesame oil
- five-spice powder
- neutral oil with a high smoke point
- white pepper if you have it, black if you don't

## Sunday

1. Marinade: {160 g / 0.5 cup} hoisin, {63 g / 3 tbsp} honey, {54 g / 3 tbsp} soy, {14 g / 1 tbsp} sesame oil, {2.5 g / 1 tsp} five-spice, grated garlic and ginger. Coat the pork strips; reserve a few spoonfuls of marinade.
2. Roast on a rack at 375°F about 90 minutes, basting with the reserved marinade twice in the back half, until burnished and 190°F for sliceable-tender. Broil 2 minutes at the end for lacquered edges. Cool, slice half-moons, box.
3. Cook the rice, spread it on a sheet pan to cool fast, then fridge it uncovered an hour before boxing. Dry rice is the entire fried-rice trick and Sunday is when it gets made.
4. Dice the carrots. That's the mise; the cooking happens on eating night.

## Weeknight

- Everything within arm's reach; the wok waits for no one. Highest heat, oil shimmering to smoking.
- Half batches, always: eggs first, soft-scrambled and out. Carrots 60 seconds, garlic-ginger 15, cold rice in — press, sear, toss, 2 minutes until it pops. Peas, pork, eggs back in, soy around the rim of the wok so it toasts on the metal, sesame oil, white pepper. Green onions off heat. Repeat for batch two.
- Extra char siu on top of each bowl, because you roasted extra and you know it.

## Notes

- Batch one waits under foil for batch two — a crowded wok steams, and steamed fried rice is just Tuesday rice.
- Kendra version: extra peas and carrots in her batch, lighter rice. The wok does not judge.
- Day 5 check: the rice is better day 3 than day 1, and the glazed pork holds all week. The fried rice itself is a cook-tonight, eat-tonight event.
