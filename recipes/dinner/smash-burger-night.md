Sunday does the boring parts — portioned beef balls, burger sauce, toppings boxed — so a weeknight double smash takes twelve minutes and zero decisions. Smash burgers are the rare meal that cannot be prepped ahead and does not need to be: the cooking is the fast part, and it is the best ten minutes the flat top sees all week. Lacy edges, cheese folded over, sauce on a toasted bun.

## Ingredients

- 3 lb | ground beef | meat | 80/20 if your Costco carries it; the leaner Kirkland works with a knob of butter on the griddle
- 6 | brioche buns | bakery | the Costco bakery pack
- 12 slices | american cheese | dairy-eggs | this is not the night for good cheddar; american melts like it loves you
- 1 head | butter lettuce | produce
- 2 | roma tomatoes | produce
- 1 | red onions | produce
- 1 jar | dill pickle chips | pantry
- 1 | salad kit | produce | the token vegetable, and a good one

### Pantry staples

- kosher salt and black pepper
- mayonnaise, ketchup, yellow mustard — plus a spoon of pickle brine and smoked paprika for the sauce
- butter, for the buns and lean-beef insurance

## Sunday

1. Portion the beef into {12} balls of 115 g each: divide the pack with a scale, then shape each portion by cupping it loosely between your palms — two or three gentle rolls, no squeezing, no seasoning. Working the meat makes burgers bouncy; a loose ball smashes into a lacy patty. Set the balls in a parchment-lined container, stack a second layer with parchment between so they do not fuse, lid, and refrigerate.
2. Burger sauce: stir {110 g} mayo, {34 g} ketchup, and {15 g} mustard together in a jar with a spoon of pickle brine, a pinch of smoked paprika, and a few grinds of black pepper. Taste it — tangy and a little smoky is right. Lid it and refrigerate.
3. That's it. Twenty-five minutes including cleanup. This recipe exists so one dinner each week costs Sunday almost nothing — the real cooking happens on burger night.

## Weeknight

- Turn the flat top to maximum and give it a full ten minutes of preheat — the sear budget is stored in the metal, and a half-hot griddle makes gray burgers. Leave the beef balls in the fridge until the moment they go on; the smash only works on cold meat.
- While it heats, set out the toppings: slice the tomatoes into thin rounds and the red onion into thin rings, pull apart the lettuce leaves, fish out a pile of pickle chips, and put the sauce jar on the counter with a spoon. Dump the salad kit into a bowl and toss it with its dressing packet.
- Toast the buns: butter the cut faces, lay them butter-side down on the cooler edge of the griddle, and check at 1 minute — you want golden brown, not blackened. Set them aside on a plate.
- Cook in batches with room to spare: lay a few balls on the griddle a hand-width apart, then smash immediately — press each ball hard with a stiff spatula, a second spatula stacked on top for leverage, until the patty is wider than a bun and thin enough that the edges look ragged. Commit within the first 30 seconds while the meat is still cold; cold beef flattens and stays put, but once it starts cooking it fights back and you lose the crust.
- Salt and pepper the smashed patties generously and leave them alone about 90 seconds. The undersides are building a deep brown crust and the edges are going lacy — when the edges look crisp and the tops start turning gray, flip time.
- Flip with a scrape: angle the spatula low and shove it hard under each patty so the crust comes up attached — that crust is the entire point of a smash burger, and a timid flip leaves it welded to the griddle. Lay a slice of american cheese on each patty as soon as it turns and give it about 45 seconds, until the cheese droops over the edges. A patty this thin is cooked through by then — no guesswork.
- Stack the patties in pairs for doubles. Sauce both cut faces of each bun, then build: bottom bun, lettuce, tomato, onion, double patty, pickles, top bun. Dinner in twelve minutes.

## Notes

- The smash only works within the first 30 seconds while the meat is cold; commit.
- Kendra version: single instead of double, extra salad. The single is still a real burger.
- Day 5 check: raw beef balls should be cooked by day 3 or moved to the freezer Sunday and thawed the night before — schedule burger night accordingly. The prepped toppings and sauce keep all week.
