Chicken thighs in a coconut red curry with peppers, zucchini, and basil, over jasmine rice. Two techniques carry the batch-cooking version: fry the curry paste in the thick coconut cream until it splits and smells like the restaurant, and pull the vegetables a notch underdone on Sunday so Thursday's reheat lands them exactly right.

## Ingredients

- 2.5 lb | boneless skinless chicken thighs | meat | cut into big bite-size pieces
- 4 oz | thai red curry paste | other-store | Mae Ploy or Maesri; supermarket Thai Kitchen is meeker, use half again as much
- 3 cans | coconut milk | pantry | full fat, the Costco 6-pack
- 2 | bell peppers | produce
- 2 | zucchini | produce
- 1 | yellow onions | produce
- 1 bunch | basil | produce | thai basil if you spot it, and grab makrut lime leaves for the freezer while you are at that market
- 2 | limes | produce
- 465 g | jasmine rice | pantry | measured dry; about 2.5 cups

### Pantry staples

- fish sauce
- brown sugar
- neutral oil

## Sunday

1. Open the coconut cans without shaking; spoon the thick cream off the top of one into a wide hot pot. Fry it until the oil separates, then fry the curry paste in that split cream 2 to 3 minutes until it darkens and stops smelling raw.
2. Chicken in, tossed to coat, 2 minutes. Then the rest of the coconut milk, {36 g / 2 tbsp} fish sauce, and {14 g / 1 tbsp} brown sugar. Simmer 12 minutes.
3. Add onion petals, pepper strips, and thick zucchini half-moons; simmer 4 minutes only — they should still argue back a little. Off heat: juice of 1 lime and most of the basil.
4. Cook the rice. Cool both, pack in dinner-size containers, curry and rice separate.

## Weeknight

- Microwave both, or reheat the curry in the wok over medium — it wakes up the paste. Fresh basil and the second lime if you have them standing by.

## Notes

- Taste your curry paste tub once and calibrate; they range from polite to volcanic.
- Kendra version: broth-forward bowl — more sauce, more vegetables, half rice.
- Day 5 check: coconut curries hold; the underdone-vegetable trick is what keeps Friday from mush.
