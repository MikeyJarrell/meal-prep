Gochujang-glazed ground beef over rice with sesame spinach and quick-pickled cucumbers — sweet, hot, garlicky, and built for the microwave. Ground beef is the honest choice for a lunch that reheats daily: it has no medium-rare to lose.

## Ingredients

- 2 lb | ground beef | meat
- 6 cloves | garlic | produce
- 30 g | fresh ginger | produce
- 60 g | gochujang | pantry | Costco usually has the bibigo tub; any Korean brand works
- 465 g | jasmine rice | pantry | measured dry
- 6 | persian cucumbers | produce
- 285 g | baby spinach | produce | the 1-pound box; the rest goes in eggs or smoothies
- 1 bunch | green onions | produce

### Pantry staples

- soy sauce
- brown sugar
- toasted sesame oil
- rice vinegar and sugar, for the pickles
- sesame seeds
- neutral oil

## Sunday

1. Pickles first, so they get the longest soak: slice the cucumbers into coins about as thick as a quarter (no need to peel) and pile them into a jar or container with a lid. In a small saucepan, heat {180 g} rice vinegar, {90 g} water, {40 g} sugar, and {8 g} salt over medium heat, stirring, until the sugar dissolves, about 2 minutes — steaming is enough, it does not need to boil. Pour the hot brine over the cucumbers, put the lid on, and refrigerate. They pickle themselves from here and are ready by packing time.
2. Cook the rice: put {465 g} jasmine rice in a strainer and rinse under cold water for about 30 seconds. Rice cooker: rice plus {580 g} water, press start. Pot: rice and {580 g} water in a lidded pot, bring to a boil over high heat, then cover, turn the heat to its lowest setting, and cook 15 minutes; turn off the heat and leave it covered 10 more minutes. Do not lift the lid until the very end.
3. While the rice cooks, prep the aromatics: peel the ginger (scraping with the edge of a spoon takes the skin off easily) and grate it. Peel the garlic; grate 1 clove and set it aside for the spinach, and mince the other 5 for the beef. Slice the green onions thin.
4. Sesame spinach: heat a wide pan over medium-high heat with just enough neutral oil to film the bottom. Add the spinach a few big handfuls at a time — it will look like far too much, then collapse within about 30 seconds — stirring and adding more as space opens up. When all of it has wilted, 2 to 3 minutes total, tip it into a colander. Once it is cool enough to touch, squeeze it firmly in your hands over the sink until water stops coming out; you will end up with a ball about the size of an orange. Pull it apart a little, then toss with {14 g} sesame oil, {12 g} soy sauce, the grated garlic clove, and a big pinch of sesame seeds.
5. The beef: wipe out the pan and heat it over high heat until a drop of water sizzles off instantly. Add the ground beef, press it flat with a spatula, and leave it alone for 2 to 3 minutes so the underside browns hard. Then break it into crumbles and cook until no pink remains. If a lot of fat has pooled, spoon most of it off — a little left behind is flavor. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger and stir for 1 minute. Stir together {90 g} soy sauce, {42 g} brown sugar, {60 g} gochujang, and {14 g} sesame oil in a small bowl, pour it over the beef, and simmer for about 3 minutes, stirring, until the sauce thickens enough to coat the meat and turn glossy instead of watery. That glossy coat is the glaze, and it is what keeps the beef good all week.
6. Pack six containers: rice across the bottom, beef and its glaze over one half, spinach beside it, and pickles in the last corner — or in small sauce cups, which keeps them cold and crunchy at reheat time. Scatter green onions and sesame seeds over everything. Leave the lids off until the food stops steaming, about 20 minutes, then lid and refrigerate.

## Weeknight

- Take the pickles out (easy if they are in a sauce cup), microwave the container 2 to 3 minutes, and add the pickles back after. Cold, crunchy pickles against hot glazed beef is the point of the whole recipe.

## Notes

- The glaze on the beef is what keeps day-4 microwaving kind; do not skimp the simmer.
- Kendra version: half rice, double spinach and pickles. The beef carries either ratio.
- A fried egg on top at home turns leftovers of this into dinner. Just saying.
