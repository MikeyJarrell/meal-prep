Shredded rotisserie chicken over a cabbage crunch salad with edamame and a peanut-lime dressing that lives in its own jar until the moment of eating. The fastest Sunday job in the library — no cooking beyond pulling a chicken apart — and the salad is all sturdy vegetables, so Friday's is as loud as Monday's.

## Ingredients

- 1 | rotisserie chicken | meat | the famous bird; about 4 cups shredded
- 0.5 head | green cabbage | produce
- 3 | carrots | produce
- 2 | bell peppers | produce
- 10 oz | frozen shelled edamame | frozen | thawed, straight into the salad
- 1 bunch | green onions | produce
- 1 bunch | cilantro | produce
- 0.75 cup | roasted peanuts | pantry
- 4 | limes | produce
- 1 oz | fresh ginger | produce

### Pantry staples

- peanut butter
- soy sauce
- toasted sesame oil
- honey
- rice vinegar
- chili flakes or sriracha

## Sunday

1. Pull the chicken while it is still warm — it shreds in half the time. Big rough shreds, skin optional but encouraged.
2. Shred the cabbage thin, julienne or coarsely grate the carrots, slice the peppers thin, slice the green onions. Toss with the thawed edamame.
3. Dressing: whisk 0.75 cup peanut butter, juice of 4 limes, 4 tablespoons soy sauce, 2 tablespoons honey, 1 tablespoon sesame oil, 1 tablespoon rice vinegar, grated ginger, and warm water until it pours like cream. Chili to taste. Portion into six jars.
4. Pack: salad base, chicken on top, cilantro and peanuts in a corner, dressing jar on the side.

## Weeknight

- Shake the jar, pour, toss, done. No microwave anywhere near it.

## Notes

- The dressing thickens in the fridge; ten seconds of shaking brings it back.
- Kendra version: as written — this is the "not super heavy" archetype. Mikey version: two scoops of chicken and a bigger pour of dressing.
- Day 5 check: cabbage, carrots, and peppers shrug off a week. This is the safest Friday lunch in the repo.
