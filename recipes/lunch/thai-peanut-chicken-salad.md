Shredded rotisserie chicken over a cabbage crunch salad with edamame and a peanut-lime dressing that lives in its own jar until the moment of eating. The fastest Sunday job in the library — no cooking beyond pulling a chicken apart — and the salad is all sturdy vegetables, so Friday's is as loud as Monday's.

## Ingredients

- 1 | rotisserie chicken | meat | the famous bird; about 500 g of meat once shredded
- 0.5 head | green cabbage | produce
- 3 | carrots | produce
- 2 | bell peppers | produce
- 285 g | frozen shelled edamame | frozen | thawed, straight into the salad
- 1 bunch | green onions | produce
- 1 bunch | cilantro | produce
- 110 g | roasted peanuts | pantry
- 4 | limes | produce
- 30 g | fresh ginger | produce

### Pantry staples

- peanut butter
- soy sauce
- toasted sesame oil
- honey
- rice vinegar
- chili flakes or sriracha

## Sunday

1. Shred the chicken while it is still warm from the store — warm meat pulls apart in half the time. Pull off the skin (chop some in if you like it, or discard it), then pull all the meat off the bones with your hands or two forks into big rough shreds. Set the meat aside; the carcass can go in the trash or into a freezer bag for stock.
2. Cut the vegetables: cut the core out of the cabbage half and slice it crosswise into thin ribbons, about as wide as a shoelace. Julienne the carrots — that means cutting them into thin matchsticks: peel them, cut into finger-length pieces, slice each piece into thin planks, stack the planks, and slice into skinny sticks — or just grate them on the big holes of a box grater, which is faster and works fine here. Halve the bell peppers, pull out the stems and seeds, and slice into thin strips. Slice the green onions thin, white and green parts both. Toss all of it in your biggest bowl with the edamame (if they are still frozen, run them under cold water in a strainer for a minute to thaw).
3. Make the dressing: peel the ginger (scraping with the edge of a spoon takes the skin off easily) and grate it. In a medium bowl, whisk {195 g} peanut butter, the juice of {4} limes, {72 g} soy sauce, {42 g} honey, {14 g} sesame oil, {15 g} rice vinegar, and the grated ginger. It will stiffen and look clumpy at first — that is normal. Whisk in {60 g} warm water a little at a time until the dressing pours off the whisk like heavy cream, adding a splash more water if it stays thick. Add chili flakes or sriracha a little at a time, tasting as you go. Divide the dressing into six small jars or sauce cups with lids; it thickens as it sits in the fridge, and a good shake brings it back.
4. Pack six containers: salad base across the bottom, chicken on top, roughly chopped cilantro and a small pile of peanuts in a corner, dressing jar on the side. The dressing only touches the salad at lunchtime — that separation is what keeps Friday's salad as crunchy as Monday's.

## Weeknight

- Shake the dressing jar hard for about 10 seconds until smooth (it thickens in the fridge), pour it over, toss everything together, and eat. No microwave anywhere near it.
