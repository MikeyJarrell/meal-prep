Firm little lentils with bacon, sweated shallots and carrots, a sharp mustard vinaigrette, goat cheese, and walnuts, with arugula folded in when you pack it. This is bistro lunch in a glass container: it eats cold, it holds all week, and the vinaigrette soaks in rather than wearing off. The salad that made Kendra say "not super heavy" sound like a spec instead of a complaint.

## Ingredients

- 2 cups | french green lentils | other-store | du Puy style; brown lentils work but go softer, so pull them 5 minutes earlier
- 0.5 lb | thick-cut bacon | meat
- 2 | shallots | produce
- 2 | carrots | produce
- 2 ribs | celery | produce
- 6 oz | goat cheese | dairy-eggs | Costco sells a two-log pack
- 1 bunch | flat-leaf parsley | produce
- 0.75 cup | walnuts | pantry | the Kirkland bag lasts a year of these
- 5 oz | arugula | produce

### Pantry staples

- olive oil
- dijon mustard
- sherry vinegar (red wine vinegar works)
- kosher salt and black pepper
- bay leaf

## Sunday

1. Simmer the lentils with a bay leaf and a garlic clove if one is rolling around, 22 to 25 minutes — they should hold their shape with a slight bite. Salt in the last 5 minutes. Drain.
2. Meanwhile cut the bacon into batons and render until crisp. Scoop out, and pour off all but a tablespoon of fat.
3. In that fat: finely diced shallots, carrots, and celery, 5 minutes — softened but not browned.
4. Vinaigrette in the bottom of the big bowl: 2 tablespoons dijon, 3 tablespoons sherry vinegar, 6 tablespoons olive oil, salt, lots of black pepper.
5. Fold in the warm lentils (warm legumes drink dressing), the vegetables, bacon, chopped parsley, and toasted walnuts. Taste — lentils absorb salt overnight, so it should taste slightly aggressive today.
6. Pack six portions; crumble goat cheese over each and lay a handful of arugula on top without mixing.

## Weeknight

- No microwave. Toss the arugula through when you eat; ten minutes out of the fridge wakes the flavors up.

## Notes

- The slightly-too-sharp-on-Sunday calibration is the whole trick; day 3 it lands perfect.
- Kendra version: as written. Mikey version: it takes a soft-boiled egg or leftover grilled chicken without complaint.
- Day 5 check: lentils are famously the best-holding legume; Friday is fine.
