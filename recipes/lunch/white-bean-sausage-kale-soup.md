Italian sausage, cannellini beans, and lacinato kale in a parmesan-enriched broth, finished with lemon so it stays lively. Soup is the microwave's one true love: it cannot dry out, it cannot toughen, and by Thursday the beans have drunk enough broth to make it nearly a stew. Pack it with bread if you have it and feel smug at noon.

## Ingredients

- 1.5 lb | mild italian sausage | meat | out of the casings if they came in links
- 3 cans | cannellini beans | pantry
- 1 | yellow onions | produce
- 3 | carrots | produce
- 3 ribs | celery | produce
- 6 cloves | garlic | produce
- 8 cups | chicken stock | pantry | two quarts
- 1 bunch | lacinato kale | produce | stemmed and torn
- 2 oz | parmigiano reggiano | dairy-eggs | plus the rind if you keep them — it goes in the pot
- 1 | lemons | produce

### Pantry staples

- olive oil
- kosher salt and black pepper
- chili flakes
- bay leaf

## Sunday

1. Brown the sausage hard in a big pot, breaking it into rubble. Take your time here — the fond on the bottom is the soup's backbone. Scoop it out.
2. In the fat: diced onion, carrot, and celery with a pinch of salt, 8 minutes. Garlic and chili flakes, one minute.
3. Stock, beans with their liquid, bay leaf, the parmesan rind, and the sausage back in. Simmer 20 minutes.
4. Stir in the kale until it collapses, about 3 minutes. Off heat: juice of the lemon, black pepper, and the grated parmesan. Taste for salt — stock varies.
5. Cool the pot in a sink of cold water for 15 minutes before portioning; six containers, and freeze two of them for Thursday and Friday.

## Weeknight

- Microwave 3 minutes, stir halfway. Frozen ones move to the fridge two nights before.

## Notes

- Mash a ladle of beans against the pot wall in step 3 if you like it thicker.
- Kendra version: naturally light despite the sausage — broth does a lot of visual heavy lifting.
- Day 5 check: the freeze-two-portions move makes Friday taste like Monday.
