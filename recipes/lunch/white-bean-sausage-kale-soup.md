Italian sausage, cannellini beans, and lacinato kale in a parmesan-enriched broth, finished with lemon so it stays lively. Soup is the microwave's one true love: it cannot dry out, it cannot toughen, and by Thursday the beans have drunk enough broth to make it nearly a stew. Pack it with bread if you have it and feel smug at noon.

## Ingredients

- 1.5 lb | mild italian sausage | meat | out of the casings if they came in links
- 3 cans | cannellini beans | pantry
- 1 | yellow onions | produce
- 3 | carrots | produce
- 3 ribs | celery | produce
- 6 cloves | garlic | produce
- 1920 g | chicken stock | pantry | two quart boxes
- 1 bunch | lacinato kale | produce | stemmed and torn
- 55 g | parmigiano reggiano | dairy-eggs | plus the rind if you keep them — it goes in the pot
- 1 | lemons | produce

### Pantry staples

- olive oil
- kosher salt and black pepper
- chili flakes
- bay leaf

## Sunday

1. Brown the sausage: set a big soup pot over medium-high heat with a glug of olive oil. Add the sausage (squeezed out of its casings if it came in links), press it flat with a spoon, and leave it alone 2 to 3 minutes so the underside browns hard. Then break it into rubble and keep cooking, stirring occasionally, until the pieces are deeply browned all over, 8 to 10 minutes total. A browned crust will build on the bottom of the pot — that crust is called fond, and it is the soup's backbone: it dissolves into the broth later and flavors everything, so let it get good and brown but not black. Scoop the sausage into a bowl, leaving the fat in the pot.
2. Cook the vegetables in that fat: turn the heat down to medium and add the onion, carrots, and celery, all diced small, with a pinch of salt. Cook, stirring now and then, about 8 minutes, until the onion turns soft and see-through. Add the garlic, minced, and a pinch of chili flakes, and stir for one minute, until the garlic smells fragrant.
3. Pour in the stock, scraping the bottom of the pot with your spoon as it goes in — the hot liquid dissolves the fond off the bottom (this is deglazing) and carries all that browned flavor into the broth. Add the beans with the liquid from their cans, the bay leaf, the parmesan rind if you have one (it never melts; it just sits in the pot slowly giving its savory flavor to the broth, like a soup bone, and gets fished out at the end), and the sausage. Bring it to a boil over high heat, then turn the heat down until it simmers — small lazy bubbles, not a churn — and cook 20 minutes. While it simmers, strip the kale leaves off their stems and tear them into bite-size pieces, grate the parmesan, and juice the lemon.
4. Stir in the kale a handful at a time and cook until it collapses and turns dark green, about 3 minutes. Turn off the heat and fish out the bay leaf and the rind. Stir in the lemon juice, plenty of black pepper, and the grated parmesan. Taste a spoonful and add salt if it needs it — stock varies, so it might need a good pinch or none at all.
5. Cool the pot fast: fill the sink with a few inches of cold water and set the pot in it for 15 minutes, stirring once or twice — the water pulls heat out far faster than air, so the soup is safe to refrigerate sooner. Portion into six containers, freeze two of them for Thursday and Friday, and refrigerate the rest.

## Weeknight

- Microwave 3 minutes, stopping to stir halfway so it heats evenly. Move each frozen container to the fridge two nights before you need it so it has time to thaw.

## Notes

- Mash a ladle of beans against the pot wall in step 3 if you like it thicker.
- Kendra version: naturally light despite the sausage — broth does a lot of visual heavy lifting.
- Day 5 check: the freeze-two-portions move makes Friday taste like Monday.
