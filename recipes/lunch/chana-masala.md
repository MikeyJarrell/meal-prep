Chickpeas simmered in a deeply browned onion-tomato masala, over cumin rice with yogurt and cilantro. The cheapest lunch in the library and the one that improves most stubbornly with age — the Friday container is the best one, which is exactly backwards from most food and exactly what a meal-prep recipe should do.

## Ingredients

- 4 cans | chickpeas | pantry
- 3 | yellow onions | produce
- 8 cloves | garlic | produce
- 55 g | fresh ginger | produce
- 1 can | crushed tomatoes (800 g can) | pantry
- 465 g | jasmine rice | pantry | measured dry; basmati if you have it around
- 1 bunch | cilantro | produce
- 2 | lemons | produce
- 250 g | plain greek yogurt | dairy-eggs
- 1 | jalapeños | produce

### Pantry staples

- neutral oil
- kosher salt
- cumin seeds and ground cumin
- ground coriander, turmeric, garam masala, cayenne
- sugar

## Sunday

1. Prep everything before the stove goes on, because the onions will need your full attention: dice the onions fine, into pieces the size of a pea or smaller. Peel the garlic and ginger (the edge of a spoon scrapes ginger skin off easily) and grate both. Halve the jalapeño, scrape out the seeds for a milder pot or leave them in for heat, and mince it fine. Drain the chickpeas, saving the liquid from the cans. Cut one lemon into six wedges and set them aside for packing.
2. Brown the onions — this step is the recipe: set a wide, heavy pot over medium-high heat with a generous pour of neutral oil, enough to coat the bottom well. Add the onions and a pinch of salt and cook, stirring every minute or two. At 8 minutes they will look golden — keep going. Around 12 minutes they will start catching real brown at the edges. Somewhere between 15 and 18 minutes they will be an even, deep brown all over and smell sweet and nutty, not burnt — that is the color you want. If dark spots race ahead of the rest, turn the heat down a notch and add a small splash of water, scraping the browned bits off the bottom as it sizzles away.
3. Add the garlic, ginger, and jalapeño and cook, stirring, 2 minutes, until the raw garlic smell fades. Add {4 g} ground cumin, {3.5 g} coriander, {3 g} turmeric, and cayenne to taste, and stir constantly for 30 seconds — this is blooming the spices: frying ground spices briefly in hot oil to wake up their flavor. They will smell strong and toasty almost instantly; do not let them sit still and scorch.
4. Add the crushed tomatoes and a pinch of sugar and cook over medium heat, stirring now and then, about 8 minutes, until the masala darkens and thickens and the oil separates at the edges — you will see a slick of orange-tinted oil pooling at the rim of the sauce and in the trail your spoon leaves. That oil is the sign the tomatoes have fully cooked down; do not stop before you see it.
5. Add the chickpeas with about {240 g} of their saved can liquid, bring back to a gentle simmer (small, lazy bubbles), and cook 15 minutes, stirring occasionally and mashing a few chickpeas against the side of the pot with your spoon to thicken the gravy. Turn off the heat and stir in {2 g} garam masala and the juice of {1} lemon. Taste and add salt until it tastes vivid rather than flat.
6. Jeera rice, which you can start while the chickpeas simmer: put {465 g} jasmine rice in a strainer and rinse under cold water for about 30 seconds. Set the rice pot over medium heat with a glug of neutral oil, add {6 g} cumin seeds, and let them sizzle until they crackle and darken a shade, about 30 seconds — blooming again, whole spices this time. Add the rice and {580 g} water, bring to a boil over high heat, then cover, turn the heat to its lowest setting, and cook 15 minutes; turn off the heat and leave it covered 10 more minutes without lifting the lid. (Rice cooker: bloom the seeds in a small pan, tip them into the cooker with the rice and water, and press start.)
7. Pack six containers: rice across the bottom, chana over it, chopped cilantro on top, a lemon wedge tucked into each, and the yogurt divided into six sauce cups. Leave the lids off until the food stops steaming, about 20 minutes, then lid and refrigerate.

## Weeknight

- Take the yogurt cup out, microwave the container 3 minutes until hot in the middle, then stir the yogurt through and squeeze the lemon wedge over.

## Notes

- Onion browning cannot be rushed on high heat without bitterness; put a podcast on.
- Kendra version: as written. Mikey version: it happily absorbs a scoop of leftover shawarma chicken or a fried egg.
- Day 5 check: the champion. Make this one early in the rotation and eat it late.
