Crisped harissa chickpeas and a sheet pan of deeply roasted vegetables over lemony quinoa, with feta and a garlicky yogurt sauce. One of the two vegetarian lunches, and the one that converts vegetable skeptics — the trick is roasting hotter and longer than feels polite, so everything caramelizes instead of steaming.

## Ingredients

- 3 cans | chickpeas | pantry | from the Costco 8-pack
- 4 oz | harissa | other-store | one small jar; brands run mild to scary, taste yours first
- 3 | zucchini | produce
- 2 | bell peppers | produce
- 2 | red onions | produce
- 1 pint | cherry tomatoes | produce
- 2 cups | quinoa | pantry | measured dry; the big Costco bag
- 3 | lemons | produce
- 1 bunch | flat-leaf parsley | produce
- 6 oz | feta | dairy-eggs
- 1 cup | plain greek yogurt | dairy-eggs
- 2 cloves | garlic | produce

### Pantry staples

- olive oil
- kosher salt and black pepper
- ground cumin and smoked paprika
- honey

## Sunday

1. Oven to 450°F, two sheet pans inside while it heats — starting vegetables on hot metal is the difference between roasted and stewed.
2. Drain the chickpeas and dry them hard on a towel. Toss with olive oil, harissa, cumin, and salt.
3. Cut zucchini into thick half-moons, peppers into wide strips, red onions into wedges. Toss with olive oil, salt, and smoked paprika. Cherry tomatoes stay whole.
4. Chickpeas on one hot pan, vegetables on the other. Roast about 25 minutes, shaking once, until the chickpeas crackle and the vegetables have real color at the edges.
5. Meanwhile cook the quinoa; fold in the zest and juice of 2 lemons, olive oil, and salt while warm.
6. Yogurt sauce: yogurt, 2 grated garlic cloves, juice of the last lemon, salt, a thread of honey. Six sauce cups.
7. Pack: quinoa, vegetables, chickpeas, feta and parsley in a corner, sauce cup on the side. Cool before lidding.

## Weeknight

- Microwave 2 minutes with the sauce cup out, or eat at room temperature — both are legitimate.

## Notes

- The chickpeas de-crisp in the fridge no matter what you do; they trade crunch for chew and are still good. Accept it.
- Kendra version: as written. Mikey version: add a grilled thigh from the shawarma batch or a scoop of leftover tinga — the bowls take passengers happily.
- Day 5 check: everything holds; the tomatoes get jammier, which is a feature.
