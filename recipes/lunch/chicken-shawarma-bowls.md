Yogurt-marinated thighs blasted on a hot grill until the edges char, sliced over turmeric rice with a chopped cucumber-tomato salad, quick-pickled cabbage, and lemon-tahini sauce. The grill is already lit on Sunday for whatever dinner you picked, so this rides along. Everything except the rice is genuinely good cold, which makes it the bowl to grab when the microwave line at school is long.

## Ingredients

- 3 lb | boneless skinless chicken thighs | meat | the other half of the Kirkland pack
- 250 g | plain greek yogurt | dairy-eggs | from the big Costco tub
- 3 | lemons | produce
- 8 cloves | garlic | produce
- 465 g | jasmine rice | pantry | measured dry; turmeric goes in the pot
- 6 | persian cucumbers | produce
- 4 | roma tomatoes | produce
- 1 bunch | flat-leaf parsley | produce
- 0.5 head | green cabbage | produce | for the quick pickle
- 120 g | tahini | pantry | Costco usually stocks a good jar

### Pantry staples

- olive oil
- kosher salt and black pepper
- ground cumin, ground coriander, smoked paprika, turmeric, cinnamon
- red wine vinegar and sugar, for the cabbage
- cayenne, if you want heat

## Sunday

1. Make the marinade first — in the morning if you can, otherwise the moment you start cooking: in a large bowl, whisk the yogurt with a glug of olive oil, the juice of {1} lemon, grated garlic ({6} cloves, rubbed across the small holes of a grater), {4 g} each of cumin and coriander, {4.5 g} smoked paprika, {3 g} turmeric, a big pinch of cinnamon, and {15 g} salt. Add the thighs and turn them with your hands until every piece is coated — this is marinating, letting the meat sit in a seasoned coating so flavor soaks in, and the yogurt also tenderizes the chicken and helps the edges char on the grill. Cover the bowl and refrigerate. Even 45 minutes helps; overnight is better.
2. Quick-pickle the cabbage: cut out the hard white core, slice the cabbage into thin shreds, and pack them into a jar or container with a lid. In a small saucepan, heat {180 g} red wine vinegar, {90 g} water, {40 g} sugar, and {8 g} salt over medium heat, stirring, until the sugar dissolves, about 2 minutes — steaming is enough, it does not need to boil. Pour the hot brine over the cabbage, press it down so most of it is submerged, put the lid on, and refrigerate. It pickles itself from here.
3. Cook the rice: put {465 g} jasmine rice in a strainer and rinse under cold water for about 30 seconds. Rice cooker: rice, {580 g} water, {3 g} turmeric, and a knob of butter, press start. Pot: same ingredients in a lidded pot, bring to a boil over high heat, then cover, turn the heat to its lowest setting, and cook 15 minutes; turn off the heat and leave it covered 10 more minutes. Do not lift the lid until the very end. The turmeric turns the rice yellow — that is the point.
4. Grill the thighs over high heat, about 5 minutes a side, until the edges are charred dark and the chicken is done — push an instant-read thermometer into the thickest part of the biggest thigh and look for 175°F, the temperature where thigh meat turns tender instead of rubbery. Rest the thighs on a cutting board 5 minutes, then slice them crosswise into ribbons about as wide as your finger.
5. Make the salad while the chicken rests: chop the cucumbers and tomatoes into small bite-size pieces and chop the parsley leaves, and toss it all together in a bowl. If all six bowls will be eaten within 3 days, dress it now with the juice of {1} lemon, a glug of olive oil, and a pinch of salt. If the bowls need to last the whole week, pack the salad undressed and put a lemon wedge in each container instead — undressed vegetables stay crunchy until lunch, and dressed ones go soggy by Thursday.
6. Tahini sauce: in a bowl, whisk the tahini with the juice of the remaining lemon, {2} grated garlic cloves, and a pinch of salt, then whisk in cold water a splash at a time. Partway through it will clump and look broken — that is normal; keep whisking and adding small splashes of water and it relaxes into a smooth sauce about the thickness of heavy cream, pourable off a spoon. Divide it among six small jars or sauce cups.
7. Pack six containers: rice across the bottom, sliced chicken over it, salad in its own corner, pickled cabbage tucked beside the salad, and a sauce cup in each. Leave the lids off until the food stops steaming, about 20 minutes, then lid and refrigerate.

## Weeknight

- Take the sauce cup out, then microwave the container 2 minutes — or skip the microwave entirely and eat it cold; everything except the rice is genuinely good that way. Pour the tahini sauce over everything at the desk.

## Notes

- The sauce cups matter: tahini microwaved directly on rice turns to spackle.
- Kendra version: double salad, half rice — the recipe makes enough salad to allow it.
- Day 5 check: chicken and rice hold fine; the undressed-salad trick is what buys Thursday and Friday.
