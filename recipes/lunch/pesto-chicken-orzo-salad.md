Orzo and grilled chicken folded with pesto, cherry tomatoes, white beans, and parmesan, with arugula packed on top to toss in at lunch. Pasta salad has a bad reputation it earned at potlucks; this one avoids the crimes by overdressing slightly on Sunday and traveling with a lemon wedge, because cold mutes both salt and basil.

## Ingredients

- 1 lb | orzo | other-store | regular grocery store pasta aisle
- 2 lb | boneless skinless chicken thighs | meat | grilled alongside whatever else is on the Sunday grill
- 200 g | basil pesto | dairy-eggs | the Kirkland refrigerated tub is genuinely good
- 600 g | cherry tomatoes | produce
- 1 can | cannellini beans | pantry
- 140 g | arugula | produce
- 2 | lemons | produce
- 55 g | parmigiano reggiano | dairy-eggs

### Pantry staples

- olive oil
- kosher salt and black pepper
- chili flakes

## Sunday

1. Grill the chicken: sprinkle the thighs generously with salt on both sides and lay them on a hot grill over high heat. Cook about 5 minutes a side, until the outside has dark charred spots and the inside is fully cooked — cut into the thickest thigh and check that no pink remains, or push an instant-read thermometer into the thickest part and look for 175°F. Move the thighs to a cutting board and let them rest 5 minutes (resting lets the juices settle back into the meat instead of running out when you cut), then cut them into fat bite-size cubes.
2. While the chicken grills, boil the orzo: fill a large pot with water, add a small handful of salt, and bring it to a rolling boil over high heat. Add the orzo, stir once so it does not stick, and set a timer for 1 minute less than the cooking time printed on the package — pasta keeps softening as it sits, so pulling it slightly firm now means it is right by lunchtime. Drain it in a colander, spread it out on a sheet pan, drizzle a glug of olive oil over it, and toss with a spoon so the grains cool loose instead of gluing into clumps. Leave the pan out to cool while you make the dressing.
3. Make the dressing in your biggest mixing bowl: scrape in the pesto, add {27 g} olive oil and the juice of {1} lemon, and whisk until smooth — the oil and lemon thin the pesto so it coats every piece instead of sitting in thick clumps.
4. To the bowl of dressing, add the cooled orzo, the chicken, the cherry tomatoes cut in half, the beans (drained in a strainer and rinsed under cold water), and the parmesan, grated. Fold everything together with a big spoon until evenly coated, then taste and season assertively with salt, black pepper, and a pinch of chili flakes — it should taste slightly too seasoned right now, because cold mutes both salt and basil and this is eaten cold.
5. Pack six containers: divide the salad among them, lay a big handful of arugula flat on top of each portion without mixing it in (kept separate, it stays crisp until lunch), and cut the remaining lemon into six wedges so each container gets one riding along. If anything still feels warm, leave the lids off about 20 minutes, then lid and refrigerate.

## Weeknight

- Eat it cold or at room temperature — no microwave. Toss the arugula down through the salad with a fork, then squeeze the lemon wedge over everything before the first bite. That squeeze wakes the pesto back up and is doing real work by Thursday.

## Notes

- The beans are there so six servings of orzo salad actually hold two active people to dinner.
