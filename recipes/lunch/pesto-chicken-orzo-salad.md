Orzo and grilled chicken folded with pesto, cherry tomatoes, white beans, and parmesan, with arugula packed on top to toss in at lunch. Pasta salad has a bad reputation it earned at potlucks; this one avoids the crimes by overdressing slightly on Sunday and traveling with a lemon wedge, because cold mutes both salt and basil.

## Ingredients

- 1 lb | orzo | other-store | regular grocery store pasta aisle
- 2 lb | boneless skinless chicken thighs | meat | grilled alongside whatever else is on the Sunday grill
- 7 oz | basil pesto | dairy-eggs | the Kirkland refrigerated tub is genuinely good
- 2 pints | cherry tomatoes | produce
- 1 can | cannellini beans | pantry
- 5 oz | arugula | produce
- 2 | lemons | produce
- 2 oz | parmigiano reggiano | dairy-eggs

### Pantry staples

- olive oil
- kosher salt and black pepper
- chili flakes

## Sunday

1. Salt the thighs and grill over high heat until charred and cooked through, about 5 minutes a side. Rest, then cut into fat cubes.
2. Boil the orzo 1 minute shy of the package, drain, and spread on a sheet pan with a slick of olive oil to cool without clumping.
3. In the big bowl: pesto let down with {27 g / 2 tbsp} olive oil and the juice of {1} lemon so it coats instead of clumps.
4. Fold in the cooled orzo, chicken, halved cherry tomatoes, drained beans, and grated parmesan. Season assertively — cold food needs it.
5. Pack with arugula laid on top of each portion and a lemon wedge riding along.

## Weeknight

- Eat cold or room temperature; toss the arugula through and squeeze the lemon wedge over first. That squeeze is doing real work by Thursday.

## Notes

- The beans are there so six servings of orzo salad actually hold two active people to dinner.
- Kendra version: as written — tomatoes and arugula keep it from reading heavy.
- Day 5 check: fine, with the lemon trick. If the arugula wilts by Friday, it still tastes right folded through.
